Case File #001 • Piriyapol & Akaraborwon • 2024
The Missing Theory
of Crisis Leadership
Open
🚨

The world was full of crises, yet Piriyapol & Akaraborwon uncovered a startling truth: no formal theory of crisis leadership existed anywhere in the literature. The two investigators set to work, reading hundreds of prior studies to gather clues about what crisis leaders truly need. They mapped out a theoretical framework in full — defining Units, Laws of Interaction, Boundaries, and System States — then derived Propositions stating how the theory is expected to operate in the real world — without interviewing or surveying a single person. A note was left behind: "Empirical testing is a mission for the next generation of researchers."

Case File #002 • Sritanyarat & Russ-Eft • 2016
The Instrument Awaiting
Its Final Piece
Open
📋

Sritanyarat & Russ-Eft inherited a crucial academic legacy — a theoretically grounded framework of 25 Units for learners' evaluation, built carefully in prior doctoral research. Their mission was to turn those concepts into something measurable. They consulted three groups: instructors, students, and administrative staff. Then they surveyed 910 students at NIDA and ran statistical analysis until a reliable 35-item instrument emerged. Mission accomplished — though Steps 7 and 8 still await a future investigator.

Case File #003 • Upton & Egan • 2007
When the Detective Found
the Map Was Incomplete
Open
🗺️

Upton & Egan set out to build a theory of career development using Dubin's method. But mid-investigation, they uncovered a critical flaw — Dubin examines units at only one level of analysis, while career development unfolds simultaneously at the individual, team, and organisational levels. Rather than abandoning the method, they made a bold decision: they extended Dubin by integrating Multilevel Theory Building, enabling the framework to capture relationships both within and across levels. No primary data was collected — but a roadmap was left for those who follow.

🎯 The Hidden Lesson Across All Three Cases
Case #001 reveals...
Theory Before Research
Dubin always demands that theory comes first. A complete conceptual framework is a legitimate scholarly contribution, even without any data collected.
Case #002 reveals...
Structured yet Flexible
Dubin's 8 steps do not need to be completed in a single study. They can accumulate across research projects and build upon one another over time.
Case #003 reveals...
Designed for Practice — with Limits
Dubin works well for applied disciplines, but its single-level constraint is real. Researchers must know the boundaries and be willing to extend the method when the phenomenon demands it.
